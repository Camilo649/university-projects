    great visualization for `shufps`. It's hard to understand it by reading the official doc. Thanks!

    It's common to see asm code like this being produced by gcc -O3 to multiply single scalar with a vector.

    ```
    # att syntax
    movss 8(%rdi,%rax), %xmm0
    shufps $0, %xmm0, %xmm0 ## broadcasting
    movaps %xmm0, %xmm1
    mulps -64(%rbp), %xmm1 ## SIMD on 4 floats from mem
    ```

    initially, I thought there's one special SIMD instruction that won't replicate the single scalar (32 bits) into entire xmm register (128 bits) in order to do SIMD. It seems that extra broadcasting is required and such special instruction doesn't exist.
